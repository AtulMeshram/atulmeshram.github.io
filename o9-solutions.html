Role

At o9, I have designed and prototyped dozens of widgets and features for web, mobile, and desktop applications that varied from small, one-week prototypes that explored specific interactions to large-scale, multi-month projects targeted for the launch.

During this time, I owned entire features from conception to design (visual & interaction) to near-production implementation, and in a couple cases, spearheaded entire projects with product managers as well, a role which entailed a good deal of higher-level product thinking (market fit, user need, fitting into o9's existing ecosystem, etc.)

I built prototypes that varied from low-fidelity mocks and quick proof-of-concept, to complex, production-quality applications that combined dozens of features and interactions.

Within a team, my biggest asset was my ability to identify and conceptualize high-potential use cases, and quickly turnaround functional prototypes that were aesthetic, intuitive, and polished. This allowed ideas to be quickly evaluated and helped seed faster our learnings into the rest of Daydream.

Bring the right people and facts together in real time to solve problems.

Smart analytics that forecast the future and suggest the smart plays.
4

5

Drive real time alignment around decisions, monitor execution vs. plan.

AI Based Learning
Measure performance intelligently, drive accountability and continuous improvement.

Solution

Designed an AI-based smart platform that can break complex business problems into simple solutions. An intuitive, interactive, and smart analytical visual interface product platform for the users. Learnings

Designing for o9 Solutions offered me a critical learning experience, a project of many firsts. I came away with a deeper understanding of user-driven design, data-visualization, international localization, and the tricky technical parts of product design and development.

I grew as a designer and became more adept at creating complex design systems; I grew as an engineer and better understood how to architect and build large-scale systems.

But, perhaps unparalleled, were the learnings from the product side: the failures, the successes, and the in-betweens.
